# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders and CTOs at funded tech startups and scale-ups. They have a technical background or work closely with engineers. They're evaluating whether B!R is the right external team — senior enough, fast enough, and sharp enough to trust with production systems. The decision often happens in under 60 seconds of scrolling: does this feel like engineers or a design shop pretending to be engineers?

## Product Purpose

B!R Technologies (birtechnologies.com) is a software agency delivering three practices under one senior team: product design and engineering, DevOps automation, and production-grade AI agents. The site exists to convert qualified technical founders into project conversations. Success = a CTO or founder clicking "Start a Project" with confidence, not curiosity.

## Brand Personality

Direct, senior, no-BS. The voice is terse and technical — the way a staff engineer talks to a peer, not the way an agency pitches a client. No filler sentences. No adjective stacks. Confidence through precision.

Three words: **exacting, frank, sharp.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup template** — cream bg, rounded cards, gradient CTAs, the Lovable/Webflow template aesthetic. Looks like everyone else; signals no taste.
- **Big-4 consulting firm** — stiff, corporate, navy-and-white. Feels slow, expensive, and bureaucratic. B!R is the opposite: lean and fast.
- **Freelancer portfolio** — personal, scrappy, under-produced. Doesn't signal team scale or production-grade capability.
- **Overdesigned agency showreel** — all animation and no substance. Feels more art project than engineering firm. Motion should serve legibility, not perform creativity.

## Design Principles

1. **Engineering confidence, not agency theater.** Every design choice should earn trust with a technical buyer. If a decision makes the site look "designed" without also making it feel precise and intentional, cut it.
2. **Terse over verbose.** Copy, layout, and UI all say the thing once, correctly. White space is not padding — it's punctuation.
3. **The work speaks.** Portfolio and case studies carry the actual selling weight. The chrome (nav, headers, wrappers) steps back and lets content lead.
4. **Senior signal.** The site should feel like it was built by the same caliber of engineer you're hiring. No scaffolding, no defaults left in, no slop.
5. **Hard choices, no defaults.** The brutalist aesthetic is deliberate — bone paper, ink black, signal yellow, zero radius, offset shadows. These are decisions, not a starting point. Respect and extend the system; never soften it toward convention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast on all backgrounds. Keyboard navigation throughout. Screen reader support via semantic HTML and ARIA where shadcn/Radix don't already handle it. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations (already in place via `useReducedMotion`). The signal-yellow accent (#FDE000 range) on dark backgrounds should be verified against ink — yellow-on-bone is the primary risk area.
